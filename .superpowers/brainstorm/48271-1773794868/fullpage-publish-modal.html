Common Fire Hazards
What creates a fire risk?
Fire requires three elements to exist: heat, fuel, and oxygen — commonly known as the fire triangle. Understanding where hazards concentrate, and how everyday activities can bring these elements together, is the foundation of effective fire prevention.
💡 A single overloaded extension lead can carry enough heat to ignite nearby materials within minutes.
Electrical hazards in the workplace
Electrical faults account for approximately 25% of all workplace fires in the UK. Overloaded sockets, damaged cables, and equipment left on standby are the leading causes.
